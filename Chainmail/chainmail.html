Images of Chainmail Art

Fleur de Lis

My first piece. I wanted to do something martial, yet somewhat ornate. I thought a fleur de lis was appropiate.

This piece has almost 6000 rings and is composed of 22 gauge copper and dark annealed steel wire. It uses a 4:1 European weave (which was the only weave I knew at the time).

Charizard

This piece is my attempt to test how well finer details come out. Fortunately, pokemon in the old gameboy pokemon games only use 4 colors. This Charizard specifically has white, black, red, and orange (rendered in galvanized steel, dark annealed steel, copper, and brass respectively).

I think the details came out wonderfully. In fact this is still one of my favorite pieces. It is very similar to the fleur de lis in scope: about 6000 rings, uses a 4:1 weave.

Double Helix

An experimental 3D piece. The two helices have a grandient from red to white (transitioning from copper, to bronze, to brass, to steel).

I haven't quite decided the best way to hang this one, but I will update the picture when I get some better placement.

Still a 4:1 weave.

Swirl

Still unfinished. But the rings are assembled.

This time intensive piece has 216 (65536) rings and uses a 6:1 European weave. 5 colors are used to get the greyscale effect: aluminum, plain brass, 2 types of anodized brass, and dark annealed steel.

The design is based on a genetic pattern originally made for a calendar for my family.
