Chainmail Art

Inlays

Fluer de Lis

My first piece. I wanted to do something martial, yet somewhat ornate. I thought a fleur de lis was appropriate.

This piece has 5989 rings and is composed of 22 gauge copper and dark annealed steel wire. It uses a 4:1 European weave (which was the only weave I knew at the time). Notice the ARM signature in the bottom right corner.

Charizard

This piece is my attempt to test how well finer details come out. Fortunately, pokemon in the old gameboy pokemon games only use 4 colors. This Charizard specifically has white, black, red, and orange (rendered in galvanized steel, dark annealed steel, copper, and brass respectively).

I think the details came out wonderfully. In fact this is still one of my favorite pieces. It is very similar to the fleur de lis in scope: 6498 rings, uses a 4:1 weave.

Swirl

This time intensive piece has 216 (65536) rings and uses a 6:1 European weave. 5 colors are used to get the grayscale effect: aluminum, plain brass, 2 types of anodized brass, and dark annealed steel.

The design is based on a genetic pattern originally made for a calendar for my family.

Haunter

Yes, another pokemon. This Haunter was a crucial test of my ability to work with the anodized titanium wire I got from The Ring Lord. Besides what has become the standard steel and aluminum wire, this piece incorporates "violet" and "rose" colored anodized titanium and I think the effect works quite well.

The only feature that didn't come across right were Haunter's pupils, which I merely removed (replaced with aluminum rings).

Necklaces

I initially started making this necklace because I had an excess of a couple thousand plain brass rings after finishing the swirl. It is a simple 4:1 weave torus of width 4.

My first necklace received enough praise that I decided to make a follow up necklace for my wife's birthday. I used the new rods I bought to make larger rings, which are needed to capture the quartz marbles in each strand.

A simple necklace (the same as my first) with gradient from brass to black steel.

This necklace is an experiment in the effect of increasing ring size towards the edge.

I started randomly making a cylinder of Dragon mail and decided it reminded me of a snake. So I made a head and tail to match. The tongue is actually a toggle clasp that fits into the end ring of the tail.

A variant on my first necklace, this one is a 6:1 weave torus of width 3 made of aluminum.

The pendant is a Hamiltonian cycle of a tesseract, and the chain is a simple box weave, both in black steel.

As a gift for my mother I experimented with incorporating beads (jade in this case) into a necklace.

A 8:1 aluminum Mobius strip.
